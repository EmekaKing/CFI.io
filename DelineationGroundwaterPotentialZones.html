Delineation of Groundwater Potential Zones Using MCDA and GIS in Enugu South-East, Nigeria.


The main objectives of the study were to use Multi-Criteria Decision Analysis along with GIS to define, delineate and evaluate appropriate groundwater potential zones incorporating the above thematic maps to develop a GWPZone assessment. To generate the groundwater potential zones of the study area, an approach was adopted by extracting information from conventional methods on lithology, geological structures from scanned paper maps, land use/land cover and drainage networks, soil, properties, terrain slope and examining all these in an integrated manner in a ArcGIS environment.


The result showed that quite small and efficient groundwater resources are found mainly in the lower catchment area above Enugu Central. Potentially low and very low groundwater zones are found in the Mamu Complex Formation of the catchment. The moderate potential groundwater zone extends throughout the watershed and occupies 73.84% of the study area. Potential high and very low groundwater zones cover an area of 12.98% and 0.18% respectively.
