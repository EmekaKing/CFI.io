Analysing the relationship between White-fronted Goose breeding and Light Pollution.


The GitHub repo with the Scripts and ArcGIS Toolboxes are available HERE.


The main objectives of the project were to:


  1. Find a relationship between light pollution levels and Greater White-fronted Goose breeding sites;
  2. Identify patterns in distances from breeding sites to light pollution levels, and
  3. identify Greater White-fronted Goose breeding sites. This was implemented using ArcGIS notebook to analyze white-fronted goose movement data from Movebank.

The implementation included pre-processing, spatial distribution of identified White-fronted Goose breeding sites, exploratory data analysis between artificial light (nighttime light) and breeding sites in 2007 and 2008, and calculation of distances and durations from breeding and non-breeding sites to light pollution values.


This was based on GPS Tracking data of 21 eagle owls was obtained from the Movebank online database of animal tracking.
