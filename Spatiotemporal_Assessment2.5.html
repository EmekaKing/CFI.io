Spatiotemporal Assessment of Particulate Matter 2.5 (PM2.5) in Lagos State, Nigeria


The objective of the project was to determine PM2.5 concentrations over time and space in Lagos. This was achieved by geo-exploring the annual average PM2.5 concentration in Lagos State - Nigeria from 2007 to 2016 using both inverse distance weighting and kriging methods. To achieve this goal, we used several proven statistical methods that are accurate and computationally efficient to manage the 202 geospatial datasets of individual monitoring stations across the state. This research has several policy ramifications related to environmental implementation and enforcement. Spatially and temporally resolved PM2.5 forecasts can directly support air pollution monitoring. Volatility measurement of daily PM2.5 forecasts will be beneficial for environmental management decisions. We have also found our model results useful for assessing short-term and long-term population concentrations and for studying the acute and chronic health effects of PM2.5.
