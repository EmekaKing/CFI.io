Land Cover Prediction for the North of Portugal Using Meris Imagery and Machine Learning Models.


The goal of this project was to classify the point-representing pixels of all groups of the MERIS (satellite image) into defined CORINE Land Cover (CLC) nomenclature levels based on the band values using machine learning models. In this project, we used the program WEKA to try to evaluate the accuracy and performance of different algorithms as they relate to the classification of the northern part of Portugal according to CLC nomenclature. Our dataset consists of 2006 MERIS imagery acquired with 14 bands and with 64,288 pixels or data points and their corresponding CLC classes. Multilayer Perceptron proved to be the most competent and Random Forest the least competent of all the classifiers compared for all 3 CLC levels. Although the training time of Random Forest was much shorter, the trade-off between performance and training time was evaluated in favour of Multilayer Perceptron.
